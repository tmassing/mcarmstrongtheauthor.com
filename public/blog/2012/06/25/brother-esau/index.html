I have listened to Reverend Todd. I have seen the ATMs in the sanctuaries. I have seen the churches converted to gymnasiums and discotheques and police stations and museums. You cannot have a faith that can be so easily converted to so many things. This is the essence of Christianity. It is like capitalism. It is like the chameleon, and, therefore, the American was like a chameleon. Whatever shape or color he needed for a given situation he assumed. This is why so many great actors and actresses come from here—this tendency. One must be experienced in his craft in order to juggle the identity of the chameleon with the true identity of the believer.

About Me

M. C. Armstrong recently embedded with SEAL Team 4 in Haditha, Iraq. He published extensively on the Iraq war through The Winchester Star. He is the winner of a Pushcart Prize in fiction. His work has appeared in The Missouri Review, The Gettysburg Review, Dead Letters, Kearney Street Books, Mayday, and other journals and anthologies. He is the co-founder and co-editor of Convergence Review. He is the lead guitarist and vocalist for Viva la Muerte. He is currently at work on a novel about Khalid Sheikh Mohammed’s time as a student in America.
